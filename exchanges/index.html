Our fundamental guide regarding exchanges

Security and Storage

Remember, if it's not your keys, then it's not your coins.
A multitude of exchanges have had security issues in the past (FTX) and lost their users funds. Since cryptocurrency is decentralised, once assets are sent they are lost permanently. Ensure you select an exchange with a good track history and reputation. After exchanging your selected currency with bitcoin, take your coins off the exchange to your personal wallet, whether it be software or hardware.

Asset Choices and Liquidity

For an exchange to allow you to trade your currency for cryptocurrency, it must have liquidity at all times. This means there must be a high amount of users on the exchange to ensure there are enough orders avaliable for exchange. Low liquidity on an exchange could mean you aren't able to purchase your desired asset, since there are no other people avaliable to trade. Additionally, asset choices may be limited on smaller and lesser known exchanges.

Deposit and Withdrawal Caps

Exchanges often put limits on the amount users can deposit and withdraw per day, due to verification and fraudlent prevention measures. If stolen credit is deposited and quickly exchanged to cryptocurrency, and sent to a personal custodial crypto wallet outside of the exchange's control, then the exchange may be liable for the lost funds. These anti-money laundering measures make it more difficult for criminals to engage in monetary illicit activities.

Customer Support

Customer support is absolutely essential for those who are newer to the realm of cryptocurrency, as high quality customer support can provide guidance in the technical aspects of cryptocurrency. Additionally, good customer support can assist customers when they are unsure what to do with in transfering or receiving funds.
