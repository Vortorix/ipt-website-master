Things to keep in mind when selecting a wallet

Not All Wallets are made equal

It's important to keep in mind that different wallets are made for different people. Whether you wan to keep bitcoin untouched as an investment or store of value, or if you wish to spend it, differing circumstances severly impact the wallet best for you.

A Hardware Wallet or Software Wallet?

In essence, a hardware wallet is one designed to store your private keys (effectively a password for your bitcoin wallet) completely offline and isolated from the web, making it almost impossible to hack. However, they are more expensive and come at a disadvantages to convenience when making purchases and transfers.

Software wallets are kept on a device online, and consequently are more prone to attacks, but have unmatched convenience when making purchases.

Security and Transparency

Whilst some wallets are completely open source (meaning the code is viewable by anyone), others are not. Choosing an open source wallet ensures higher levels of security and transparency, unlike a closed wallet which are trust based and may have "backdoors".

Backdoors allow rebellious developers access to your wallet and funds without your knowledge. Additionaly, open source wallets are often more secure, as any developer can view the code, and report bugs and breaches they recognise.

Operating System and Use Case

Depending on your specific Operating system, you may consider a different wallet that is designed for usage in a mobile device rather than a desktop device. Usage on an android device for on-the-go transactions would require a vastly different wallet than a desktop wallet for long term cold storage.
